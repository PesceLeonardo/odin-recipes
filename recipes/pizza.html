Pizza

Pizza is an Italian dish typically consisting of a flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomato sauce cheese, and other ingredients, baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

The term pizza was first recorded in 997 AD, in a Latin manuscript from the southern Italian town of Gaeta, in Lazio, on the border with Campania. Raffaele Esposito is often credited for creating the modern pizza in Naples. In 2009, Neapolitan pizza was registered with the European Union as a traditional speciality guaranteed (TSG) dish. In 2017, the art of making Neapolitan pizza was included on UNESCO's list of intangible cultural heritage.

Pizza and its variants are among the most popular foods in the world. Pizza is sold at a variety of restaurants, including pizzerias (pizza specialty restaurants), Mediterranean restaurants, via delivery, and as street food. In Italy, pizza served in a restaurant is presented unsliced, and is eaten with the use of a knife and fork. In casual settings, however, it is typically cut into slices to be eaten while held in the hand. Pizza is also sold in grocery stores in a variety of forms, including frozen or as kits for self-assembly. Store-bought pizzas are then cooked using a home oven.

Ingredients (for the dough)

  • 355ml of warm water (40°C - 45°C)
  • 1 package (2.25 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 3 3/4 cups (490g) bread flour
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil (omit if cooking pizza in a wood-fired pizza oven)
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Ingredients (for the toppings)

  • Tomato sauce
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt
  • Oregano
  • Whatever you want

Steps

  1. Proof the Yeast

    1. Pour 175ml warm water into a large mixing bowl.
    2. Sprinkle 2¼ teaspoons (7g) active dry yeast over the water.
    3. Let it sit for 5 minutes until dissolved and foamy. Stir if needed.

  2. Make and Knead the Dough

    1. Add the following ingredients to the bowl:
      • 250g all-purpose flour
      • 1 teaspoon of salt
      • 1 teaspoon of sugar
      • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
    2. Mix on low speed using a stand mixer with a paddle attachment for 1 minute.
    3. Switch to the dough hook and knead for 7–10 minutes on low-medium speed.
      (If kneading by hand, mix and knead until smooth and slightly sticky.)
    4. If the dough is too wet, add a little more flour.

  3. Let the Dough Rise

    1. Lightly oil a large bowl with 1 teaspoon olive oil.
    2. Place the dough in the bowl, turning it to coat with oil.
    3. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rise:
      • Fast Rise: 1.5 hours in a warm place (24–29°C).
      • Medium Rise: 8 hours at room temperature.
      • Slow Rise: 24 hours in the fridge (for better flavor).

  4. Preheat the Oven and Pizza Stone

    1. Place a pizza stone or baking sheet on the lower third rack of your oven.
    2. Preheat to 245°C for at least 30 minutes (preferably 1 hour).

  5. Divide and Shape the Dough

    1. Remove the dough from the bowl and deflate it slightly.
    2. Divide into 2 equal balls.
    3. Let them rest for 15 minutes to 2 hours, covered.
    4. On a lightly floured surface, flatten one ball into a 25–30cm circle:
      • Press the center and stretch outward.
      • Let it rest 5 minutes if it resists stretching.
      • Gently pull and rotate until desired size is reached.

  6. Prepare the Dough for Toppings

    1. Press small dents into the dough to prevent bubbling.
    2. Brush the top with olive oil to avoid sogginess.
    3. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes.

  7. Transfer to Pizza Peel

    1. Lightly sprinkle a pizza peel or baking sheet with cornmeal.
    2. Place the stretched dough on top.
    3. Reshape if necessary.

  8. Add Toppings

    1. Spread 80ml tomato sauce evenly over the dough.
    2. Sprinkle with 80g shredded cheese.
    3. Add toppings sparingly to avoid a soggy crust.

  9. Bake the Pizza

    1. Sprinkle cornmeal on the hot baking stone.
    2. Slide the pizza onto the stone.
    3. Bake at 245°C for 10–15 minutes until:
      • The crust is golden brown.
      • The cheese is melted and bubbly.
    4. (Optional) Add more cheese in the last 2 minutes of baking.

  10. Serve and Enjoy!

    1. Remove the pizza from the oven.
    2. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
    3. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade pizza!
